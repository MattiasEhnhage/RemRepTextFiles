The applicable guidelines for remuneration to senior executives, adopted by the Annual General Meeting 
in May 2021, can be found on page 107 of ITAB’s 2024 Annual Report. Under the remuneration 
guidelines, remuneration to senior executives shall be on market terms and may consist of the following 
components: fixed cash salary, variable cash remuneration, pension benefits , and other benefits. The 
variable cash remuneration shall be linked to financial or non -financial criteria. They may be individualised 
quantitative or qualitative objectives. The criteria shall be designed to contribute to the Company’s 
business strategy a nd long -term interests, including its sustainability, by for example being clearly linked 
to the business strategy or promote the executive’s long -term development.
